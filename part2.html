Five ways Alan Turing changed the world

Alan Turing (1912-1954), was a British computer scientist. Nowadays, he is widely acknowledged as the father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence.

First of all, in theoretical computer science, he first proposed the concept of Turing machine, to study what kind of problems can be solved by computational machines, and rigorously proved that the halting problem cannot be calculated. This opened up the research on the computability theory, and for the first time made people realize that the range of capabilities of computers is limited, and computers cannot solve all problems, which is very fundamental for people to understand computers more deeply. Today, computability theory and computational complexity theory (to study what kinds of problems can be efficiently solved by computers in terms of time and space) are still the core research topics of the theoretical computer community.

Secondly, Turing also had pioneering contributions in artificial intelligence. He first proposed the concept of the Turing test, attempting to provide a formal definition of artificial intelligence. Turing considered that if a human interrogator cannot tell the difference between a computer machine and a human being by communicating with them separately, then the computer can be considered to achieve intelligent behaviour. To this day, the academic community is still working hard to achieve the ultimate goal of artificial intelligence.

Finally, during the World War II, Turing was responsible for participating in the deciphering of German secret military codes, and together with colleagues, he successfully deciphered the German cryptography system, Enigma, so that the Allied forces had a full grasp of German military intelligence, which played a very crucial role to ending the war early. His research on cryptanalysis has been only known after a long time due to confidentiality reasons. Cryptography is also an important branch of computer science today, and it has very important applications in Internet security, privacy, and so on.

To summarize, Turing has made groundbreaking contributions in several important computer science related fields (computability theory, artificial intelligence, and cryptography), which established his position as the founder of computer science in the history, and therefore significantly influenced the development of computer science as a whole.
